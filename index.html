What we do is:

See that 21st Century Governance is hard...

If your organisation matters - if power within your organisation has meaning in the world, then governance gets harder. If we try to avoid power, or try to suppress it, or pretend that it doesn't affect us, then it will assert itself in the shadows, and things get still harder. If we take the opposite approach; applaud power, give in to power - see it as an end in itself - then we lose our humanity.

Address reality...

Power is hardest to address in organisations that seek to avoid rigid hierarchy, that wish to erode disadvantage, that aim to decentralise. In these contexts, 'power' can be seen as dirty, and so unattended to. It is by acknowledging the existence of power, by addressing it head on, in its reality:
its uses and abuses, its psychology and anthropology, its drive and its misdirection,
that we can ensure the alignment of power with the meaning, ethics and aims of the organisation.
So that we can do the good we aim to do.
Governance achieves this through structure: social, technical and legal structure. It is in the understanding, design and implementation of these structures that we seek to be effective.

Take Governance seriously.

In the 21st Century, our technology has an unprecedented ability to confer power at scale on those who know how to wield it.
With great capabilities come great responsibilities. We think we can help.

Trevor Hilder

Trevor learned Systems Thinking from Professor Stafford Beer, the founder of Management Cybernetics, and applies it to solving complex problems with carefully selected small teams of people.

Trevor went to university in 1969 to study Theoretical Physics. But when he got there, he discovered the joys of other disciplines and emerged with a degree in Theology & Philosophy instead. He qualified as a teacher, then moved into the field of Information & Communication Technology(ICT) in 1974. He set up the first ICT training department at Tesco Headquarters in Cheshunt in 1977, then moved into systems programming, worked for a timesharing bureau, pioneered the use of micro-computers in business, wrote one of the first database systems to run on the IBM PC, and has since managed ICT projects ranging from access control systems, to an ERP system for a supermarket chain, to the management of fleets of vehicles for motor manufacturers. Trevor learned Systems Thinking from Professor Stafford Beer, the founder of ManagementCybernetics, and applies it to solving complex problems with carefully selected small teams of people. He has a deep interest in the history of science, religion and technology and how the relationship between these has evolved since the dawn of civilisation 5,000 years ago. He closely follows developments in ICT and is particularly interested in how cloud computing, mobile telephony and blockchain technology are laying the foundations for the next generation of the internet and what impact this will have on the global financial system and the world of education and work. Trevor is a STEM Ambassador to schools in England, a member of the Consultative ExpertWorking Group of The Education Relief Foundation, an NGO based in Geneva, and has been anAssociate of St George’s House, a think tank based at Windsor Castle, since 2011. He is also a member of Newspeak House, a research centre for political technologists, in Shoreditch, London. Trevor is the CEO of Nail Soup Ltd and the founder of Web of Wealth.
