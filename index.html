What we do is:

See that 21st Century Governance is hard...

If your organisation matters - if power within your organisation has meaning in the world, then governance gets harder. If we try to avoid power, or try to suppress it, or pretend that it doesn't affect us, then it will assert itself in the shadows, and things get still harder. If we take the opposite approach; applaud power, give in to power - see it as an end in itself - then we lose our humanity.

Address reality...

Power is hardest to address in organisations that seek to avoid rigid hierarchy, that wish to erode disadvantage, that aim to decentralise. In these contexts, 'power' can be seen as dirty, and so unattended to. It is by acknowledging the existence of power, by addressing it head on, in its reality:
its uses and abuses, its psychology and anthropology, its drive and its misdirection,
that we can ensure the alignment of power with the meaning, ethics and aims of the organisation.
So that we can do the good we aim to do.
Governance achieves this through structure: social, technical and legal structure. It is in the understanding, design and implementation of these structures that we seek to be effective.

Take Governance seriously.

In the 21st Century, our technology has an unprecedented ability to confer power at scale on those who know how to wield it.
With great capabilities come great responsibilities. We think we can help.
